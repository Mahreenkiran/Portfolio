PhD flagship project | Population health and prevention

Behaviour-aware Digital Twin for Type 2 Diabetes prediction and prevention

Challenge: Conventional diabetes prediction often focuses heavily on biomarkers and underrepresents behavioural, psychosocial and lifestyle determinants. The goal was to build an interpretable framework that could estimate time to diabetes onset and support preventive what-if scenarios.

0.90Concordance index
500k+UK Biobank cohort scale
18Predictors in the final survival model

Employer value: Demonstrates experience with longitudinal health data, clinical risk stratification, model validation, interpretable outputs and responsible communication of modifiable risk factors.

What I did

  • Defined analytic cohorts and prepared behavioural, lifestyle, psychosocial and health variables.
  • Built penalised Cox models and neural approaches for time-to-event risk estimation.
  • Used causal reasoning and intervention simulation to examine potential changes in sleep, diet and mental health.
  • Created risk groups and survival trajectories to make model results easier to interpret.

Key findings and tools

  • Loneliness, insomnia, mental health history, smoking and dietary behaviours contributed meaningful risk information.
  • Healthy sleep duration showed a protective association, while several psychosocial stressors increased estimated hazard.
  • Tools: Python, pandas, NumPy, lifelines, scikit-learn, neural networks, SHAP, causal inference, Matplotlib.
Research collaboration | Infectious disease analytics

Machine learning prediction of dengue infection across five districts

Challenge: Analyse a complex, imbalanced, multi-year patient dataset that combined clinical, laboratory, serological, behavioural, epidemiological and environmental information, then determine what genuinely contributed to case classification.

3,000Patient records
2019–24Six-year study period
5Endemic districts

Strong analytical judgement: The analysis did not simply celebrate a high score. It identified that serological variables drove much of the full-model performance and showed that environmental variables alone were near chance for individual-level confirmation. This distinction protects against misleading claims.

Analytical approach

  • Data-quality checks and preprocessing across more than 50 variables.
  • Class imbalance handled within training folds using SMOTE and class-weighted learning.
  • Compared Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting and Decision Tree models.
  • Used stratified cross-validation, temporal and geographic validation, feature importance and ablation analysis.

Outcome and relevance

  • Initial model comparisons produced AUC-ROC values around 0.90 to 0.94.
  • A later full-feature model reached 0.995 AUC, but sensitivity analysis showed this was substantially driven by diagnostic serology.
  • Environmental-only AUC remained approximately 0.49 to 0.52, supporting their use at population or temporal surveillance level rather than individual diagnosis.
  • Tools: Python, scikit-learn, pandas, cross-validation, SMOTE, feature importance, ablation analysis.
Explainable AI | Multidisciplinary research

Climate-aware prediction of fungicide effectiveness using XGBoost and SHAP

Challenge: Integrate field trials, laboratory results, climate variables and molecular modelling to understand how temperature, humidity and precipitation altered pesticide effectiveness.

1,000Monte Carlo simulations
3Key esterase proteins identified
-5.1Best reported docking score

Transferable value: Although this project is agricultural, it directly demonstrates skills relevant to healthcare analytics: multimodal data integration, interaction effects, explainability, simulation, uncertainty and translation of technical outputs into practical recommendations.

What I contributed

  • Prepared climate and pesticide variables, including imputation, scaling, categorical encoding and outlier checks.
  • Applied XGBoost and SHAP interaction analysis to quantify climate-dependent pesticide behaviour.
  • Used simulation to test combined environmental conditions and improve interpretability.

Key result

  • Propiconazole performed strongly, with Dose 4 producing the best disease suppression and yield improvement.
  • SHAP results separated moisture-dependent, region-specific and climate-adaptable pesticides.
  • Tools: XGBoost, SHAP, Monte Carlo simulation, AlphaFold 2, molecular docking, Python.
